Activation functions

There was no determinant information to state a correlation between the "raw" experiments and the "retina" experiments in terms of the activation functions. It can be seen that depending on the experiment, there is a tendency on getting more sigmoid functions in the raw experiments rather than in the retina experiment, but this rule is not consistent in the experiment F4 and F5.

Note that the last experiment F6 is not directly comparable with the other ones, because this is a CPPN implementation for HyperNEAT whereas the other ones are the core neural network.

Average fitnesses

In the experiments from F1 to F3 it can be seen that the average fitness is stagnated in the raw experiments but increase in their retina counterparts. Moreover, the standar deviation is bigger in the retina experiments thus giving less predictavility to the robot performance behavior.

In the experiments from F4 to F6 the standar deviation is a lot smaller but the fitness average does not seem to have a pattern-like slope. Maybe the robot needs more generations to have a more visible behavior.
